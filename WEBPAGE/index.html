WARNING! ACIDIC WATER

The obtained value of your pH readings is Lower> than the normal value.

Effects of High pH content:

  • Irrigation water with a pH outside the normal range may cause a nutritional imbalance or may contain a toxic ion.
  • While exposure to acid water does not directly kill plants, the effects of low pH conditions (leaf damage, nutrient deficiencies and mineral toxicity) are detrimental to the plants. Furthermore, acidic water liquefies essential nutrients in the soil and washes them away before they can be absorbed by plants.

Remedies for low pH Content in water:

  • To decrease the soil pH use superfine dusting or water soluble sulphur.
  • To decrease the soil pH, sulphur coated fertilizers can also be used as it is environmental friendly.

ELECTRICAL CONDUCTIVITY VALUE

EC Values from the module

WARNING!

The obtained value of your EC readings is in Warning range

Effects of High EC content:

  • The presence of high salt levels is a sign that adjustments are needed before damage shows up in plants.
  • Higher osmotic pressure around the roots prevents an efficient water absorption by the plant.

Remedies for High EC Content in water:

  • Use an open, well-drained mix and irrigate frequently to minimise increasing salt concentration as mix dries out and irrigate heavily to leach out any accumulated salt.
  • Avoid light sprinkling of leaves with saline water.

DANGER!

The obtained value of your TURBIDITY readings is in Danger range

Effects of High TURBIDITY content:

  • When turbidity is high, temperature increases
  • Warmer water holds less dissolved oxygen.

Remedies for High Turbidity Content in water:

  • Sedimentation, roughing filters.
  • Rapid and slow sand filters, chemical coagulation and flocculation.
  • Water can be stored before disinfection.
